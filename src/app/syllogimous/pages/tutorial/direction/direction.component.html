How to Play Direction

Each question will present a series of directional relationships. These premises describe where one object is located in relation to another. For example:

  • Jewelry is at East of Color
  • Tail is at West of Milk
  • Color is at South-East of Tail
  • Milk is at South-East of Jewelry

If the question provides a conclusion like "Jewelry is north of Tail," and your map shows that "Jewelry" is not north of "Tail" but rather to its east, you would click False. If the statement matches your map, click True.

The Inversion Cue

Whenever you encounter a phrase highlighted in red, it's an indication that you need to think inversely about the information presented.
