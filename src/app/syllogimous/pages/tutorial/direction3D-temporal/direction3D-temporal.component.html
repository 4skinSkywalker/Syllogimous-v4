How to Play Direction3D Temporal

Direction3D Temporal questions are spatial reasoning challenges that require you to visualize and deduce the relative positions of different objects in a three-dimensional space. Hereâ€™s how you can play:

Step 1

Each Direction3D Temporal question presents a series of statements that describe the relative positions of objects. For example:

  • Statement 1: Hammer is in the future and North of Church
  • Statement 2: Dates is in the present and North-West of Hammer
  • Statement 3: Church is in the past and South-East of Dates

These statements describe the temporal and spatial relationships between the objects.

Step 2

To solve the puzzle, try to mentally visualize the spatial and temporal layout of the objects:

Imagine positioning the objects in a three-dimensional space where directions like north and south, east and west, and temporal states like past, present, and future are clearly distinguishable.

Step 3

After understanding each object's position and their relations to each other, test your understanding with a question. For example:

Question: "Is the Church to the south-east of the Dates?"

Based on the given statements, visualize their positions to answer the question.

Step 4

Based on your analysis, click the answer that best fits your visualization.

For instance, if you believe the statement is true, select 'True'. Otherwise, select 'False'.

After making your choice, the game will confirm whether you were correct or not, helping you refine your spatial reasoning skills.

The Negation Cue

Whenever you encounter a phrase highlighted in red, it's an indication that you need to think inversely about the information presented.

Tips for Success

Use a mental map: Visualize or draw a simple sketch to track the positions of each object.

Consider all dimensions: Remember that positions can change along multiple axes (north-south, east-west, above-below, and temporal).
